One of the most common questions I get from travellers is: "When is the best time to visit Sri Lanka?" The honest answer is — it depends on where you want to go.

Sri Lanka is a small island, but it has two distinct monsoon seasons that affect different parts of the country at different times. Once you understand how they work, you can plan a trip that's sunny almost everywhere you go.

## The Two Monsoons

**South-West Monsoon (May – September)**
Brings heavy rain to the south-west coast, the hill country, and Colombo. The south and west beaches become rough and less pleasant for swimming.

**North-East Monsoon (November – January)**
Affects the north, east coast, and the Cultural Triangle. This is when the east coast beaches (Arugam Bay, Trincomalee) are at their most dramatic.

## The Best Overall Window: December to March

For first-time visitors covering the classic circuit — Colombo, Kandy, Ella, the south coast — the period from **December through March** is ideal. The south and west are dry and sunny, the hill country is cool and clear, and Yala and Minneriya national parks are accessible.

This is peak season, so book accommodation early. Prices are higher, but the weather is reliably good.

## April and May: Hidden Gems

April gets overlooked because it's between monsoons, but it's often beautiful — warm, relatively dry across most of the island, and far less crowded than peak season. The Sinhala and Tamil New Year falls in April, which is a wonderful time to experience local culture.

## June to September: The North and East Shine

While the south-west gets rain, the **east coast** is at its absolute best from June to September. Arugam Bay is one of Asia's top surf spots, and Trincomalee's beaches are world-class. Jaffna in the far north is also wonderful during this period.

## My Honest Advice

Don't let the monsoon put you off completely. Sri Lanka's rain tends to come in short, intense bursts rather than all-day drizzle. I've had guests visit in July, get rained on for an hour each afternoon, and still call it their best holiday ever.

The key is to **choose your regions wisely** based on the time of year — and to have a flexible itinerary that can adapt if the weather changes.

> "There's no bad time to visit Sri Lanka. There's just better planning." — Mifras
