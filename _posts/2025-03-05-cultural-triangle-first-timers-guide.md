If you only have time for one region in Sri Lanka, make it the Cultural Triangle.

This area in the island's dry north-central zone contains the greatest concentration of UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Asia — ancient cities, rock fortresses, cave temples, and sacred stupas that date back more than 2,000 years.

Most visitors try to squeeze it into a day. I'd recommend two, minimum. Here's how to do it justice.

## What Is the Cultural Triangle?

The name refers loosely to the triangle formed by three ancient capitals:

- **Anuradhapura** — Sri Lanka's first capital, with enormous dagobas (stupas) and sacred Bo trees
- **Polonnaruwa** — The medieval capital, with remarkably well-preserved ruins you can explore by bicycle
- **Sigiriya / Dambulla** — The most popular stops: a towering rock fortress and a hillside cave temple filled with Buddhist murals

Most travellers focus on Sigiriya and Dambulla and skip the others due to time. If you have 3+ days in the region, Polonnaruwa is absolutely worth adding.

## Sigiriya: The Rock Fortress

Sigiriya is Sri Lanka's most iconic image — a sheer granite column rising 200 metres from the jungle, topped with the ruins of a 5th-century royal palace.

**What to know before you go:**

- Climb takes 45–90 minutes depending on fitness and crowd levels
- Best in the morning (cooler, better light for photos, fewer crowds)
- Entry fee: ~$30 USD for foreigners
- There's a short section of steep, narrow staircases — not suitable for those with severe vertigo or limited mobility
- The views from the top are extraordinary

I always recommend arriving at **opening time (7 am)** to beat the tour groups that arrive around 9–10 am. The difference in experience is significant.

## Dambulla Cave Temple

Just 20 minutes from Sigiriya, the Dambulla cave complex contains five caves filled with over 150 Buddha statues and ancient ceiling murals. It's one of the best-preserved ancient sites in Sri Lanka and far less visited than Sigiriya.

**Tips:**
- Remove shoes before entering (bring socks — the floors are cool)
- Dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered)
- Hire a local guide inside — the murals have fascinating stories behind them
- Entry fee: ~$10 USD

This is a genuinely spiritual place. Visit in the early morning or late afternoon for the best light and a quieter atmosphere.

## Polonnaruwa: The Forgotten Capital

Most day-trippers from Colombo or Kandy skip Polonnaruwa because it's another 90-minute drive. That's their loss.

The ancient city is set among lakes and jungle, and most of it is best explored **by bicycle** — a completely different experience from the usual temple circuit. The stone carvings at Gal Vihara (four enormous Buddha figures carved directly into a granite face) are among the finest examples of ancient art in Asia.

**Tips:**
- Hire bicycles at the entrance gate (very affordable)
- Allow at least 3–4 hours
- Combine with a morning at Sigiriya for a full 2-day Cultural Triangle experience

## My Recommended 2-Day Itinerary

**Day 1:**
- Early morning: Sigiriya rock climb (arrive at 7 am)
- Late morning: Dambulla caves
- Afternoon: Rest, or explore the nearby Minneriya National Park (elephant gathering from June–October)
- Stay overnight near Sigiriya

**Day 2:**
- Morning: Polonnaruwa by bicycle (3–4 hours)
- Afternoon: Drive towards your next destination (Kandy, Colombo, or the coast)
